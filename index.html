Jupiter

Jupiter is the fifth and largest planet in the solar system. More than twice as massive as all the other planets combined; it is almost 318 times more massive than Earth. However, Jupiter's mass is insufficient to become a Sun-like star: for this reason, its mass should be 70-80 times greater. The planet was known to people from ancient times, which is reflected in the mythology and religious beliefs of different cultures: Mesopotamian, Babylonian, Greek and others. The modern name of Jupiter comes from the name of the ancient Roman supreme god-thunder.

A number of atmospheric phenomena on Jupiter - such as storms, lightnings, auroras - have scales that are far superior to earthly ones. A notable formation in the atmosphere is the Great Red Spot - a giant storm known since the seventeenth century. Jupiter has 79 satellites, the largest of which - Io, Europe, Ganymede and Callisto - were discovered in 1610. The last 12 were only opened in 2018 [3]. Jupiter studies are carried out with the help of terrestrial and orbital telescopes, since the 1970s, 8 NASA interplanetary vehicles were sent to the planet: "Pioneers", "Voyagers", "Galileo" and others.
